About the prize

Alfred Nobel had broad cultural interests. During his early youth, he developed his literary interests which lasted throughout his life. His library consisted of a rich and broad selection of literature in different languages. During the last years of his life, he tried his hand as an author and began writing fiction. Literature was the fourth prize area Nobel mentioned in his will.

    Selma Lagerlöf

    Nationality: Swedish

    Award year: 1909

    Prize motivation: “in appreciation of the lofty idealism, vivid imagination and spiritual perception that characterize her writings”

    Selma Lagerlöf was born in the province of Värmland , Sweden . In 1888, she went to Stockholm to study at the Royal Women's Pedagogical School. After graduation, Lagerlöf taught in Landskrona (the southernmost part of Sweden ). There she began to write her first novel The Legend of Gösta Berlings (1891), which was awarded the first prize of a literary competition and brought fame to the young female writer, from which she decided to dedicate her life to the world. his life for his literary career.

    Paul Johann Ludwig von Heyse

    Nationality: Berlin , Germany

    Award year: 1910

    Prize motivation: “as a tribute to the consummate artistry, permeated with idealism, which he has demonstrated during his long productive career as a lyric poet, dramatist, novelist and writer of world-renowned short stories”

    Paul Johann Ludwig von Heyse (March 15, 1830 – April 2, 1914). Paul Heyse studied classical literature and philology at the University of Berlin , then took a further course in Roman literature at the University of Bonn . In 1852 Heyse went to Italy to further his studies, where he began to write poetry, plays and short stories. In 1854 the king of Bavaria offered Heyse a substantial scholarship and invited him to stay at the palace in Munich.. He engaged in the intellectual life sponsored by the king, married Margaret Kugler - his teacher's daughter - and spent the rest of his life with his family in Munich, devoted to literary activities, not caring. to making a living.

    Romain Rolland

    Nationality: France

    Award year: 1915

    Prize motivation: “as a tribute to the lofty idealism of his literary production and to the sympathy and love of truth with which he has described different types of human beings”

    Romain Rolland (January 29, 1866 – December 30, 1944) was a French writer and playwright who won the 1915 Nobel Prize for Literature . After finishing high school, he studied at the History department of the École normale supérieure in Paris ; After graduating from school, he went to Italy to study art history. In 1895, he defended two doctoral theses on art history, then taught the history of painting at the École Normale Supérieure and led the music theory department at the Sorbonne University . In 1897 Rolland published the work Saint-Louis (Saint Louis) which attracted public attention, opening a separate path in literature interested in socialist and revolutionary issues.

    Carl Spitteler

    Nationality: Switzerland

    Award year: 1919

    Prize motivation: “in special appreciation of his epic, Olympian Spring”

    Carl Friedrich Georg Spitteler (pen name: Carl Felix Tandem ; April 24 , 1845 – December 29, 1924) was a Swiss poet and writer who won the Nobel Prize for Literature in 1919. In 1863 he studied at the Faculty of Law of the University of Zurich . In 1865-1870, he continued to study theology at the University of Zurich, the University of Heidelberg and the University of Basel , but then refused to become a pastor to have conditions for literary activities. In 1871-1879, Spitteler worked as a tutor to the second son of General Standertskjold in Saint Petersburg , composing the epic epic Prometheus und Epimetheus.(Prometheus and Epimetheus, 1881), then returned to Switzerland to use his own money to publish this work under the pseudonym Carl Felix Tandem
